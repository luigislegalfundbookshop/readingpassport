Science & Philosophy

The Ape that Understood the Universe: How the Mind and Culture Evolve

by Steve Stewart-Williams

A compelling look at human behavior through the lenses of evolutionary psychology and culture, exploring how our minds and societies have developed over time.

★★★★★

The Code Book: The Science of Secrecy from Ancient Egypt to Quantum Cryptography

by Simon Singh

An engaging history of cryptography, from ancient ciphers to modern-day encryption, revealing the secrets and mathematical principles behind codes and code-breaking.

★★★★

Dollars and Sense: How We Misthink Money and How to Spend Smarter

by Dan Ariely & Jeff Kreisler

This book explores the irrational ways we think about and spend money, offering practical insights and advice on how to make smarter financial decisions.

★★★

Everybody Lies: Big Data, New Data, and What the Internet Can Tell Us About Who We Really Are

by Seth Stephens-Davidowitz

A fascinating investigation into what people's anonymous online search queries reveal about their true thoughts and desires, using big data to uncover surprising truths about human behavior.

★★★★

Freakonomics: A Rogue Economist Explores the Hidden Side of Everything

by Steven D. Levitt & Stephen J. Dubner

This book applies economic principles to unconventional topics, exploring the hidden motivations behind everything from crime rates to the names people give their children.

★★★

Outliers: The Story of Success

by Malcolm Gladwell

Gladwell challenges the traditional notion of success, arguing that it's often less about innate talent and more about circumstances, culture, and opportunity.

★★★

Predictably Irrational: The Hidden Forces That Shape Our Decisions

by Dan Ariely

This book reveals the systematic and predictable ways we make irrational decisions in our daily lives, from love and money to work and happiness.

★★★★★

Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind

by Yuval Noah Harari

A sweeping history of the human species, from our evolution as hunter-gatherers to our current status as the dominant species on Earth.

★★★★★

Sapiens: A Graphic History, Volume 1 - The Birth of Humankind

by David Vandermeulen & Yuval Noah Harari

The first volume of a graphic novel adaptation of Harari's original work, detailing the early history of humankind in an accessible, illustrated format.

★★★★

Talking to Strangers: What We Should Know About the People We Don't Know

by Malcolm Gladwell

Gladwell explores the reasons why our interactions with strangers often go wrong, using high-profile cases to examine the tools we use to make sense of people we don't know.

★★★

What's Our Problem?: A Self-Help Book for Societies

by Tim Urban

This book uses a unique, long-form blog post style to diagnose the problems facing modern societies and offers a path forward by focusing on honest and constructive discourse.

★★★★★

The AI Revolution: The Road to Superintelligence

by Tim Urban

A non-technical explanation of artificial intelligence, from its current state to the potential for a superintelligent future and the profound implications that would have for humanity.

★★★★★

The Elon Musk Blog Series: Wait But Why

by Tim Urban

A collection of deeply researched blog posts that delve into the life and ventures of Elon Musk, providing a comprehensive overview of his companies like Tesla and SpaceX.

★★★★★

by Trevor Noah

The compelling memoir of Trevor Noah's childhood in apartheid-era South Africa, chronicling his journey from a young boy to a world-renowned comedian.

★★★★★

Elon Musk: Tesla, SpaceX, and the Quest for a Fantastic Future

by Ashlee Vance

The definitive biography of technology mogul Elon Musk, offering a look at his ambitious vision and the challenges he faced in building his groundbreaking companies.

★★★★

Hillbilly Elegy: A Memoir of a Family and Culture in Crisis

A personal account of the social and economic problems of the white working class in Appalachia, exploring the author's own upbringing and the cycles of poverty and instability.

Professional Idiot: A Memoir

by Stephen "Steve-O" Glover

The unfiltered memoir of Jackass star Steve-O, detailing his rise to fame, struggles with addiction, and ultimate path to recovery.

★★★★

Shoe Dog: A Memoir by the Creator of Nike

Fantasy & Science Fiction

Brave New World

by Aldous Huxley

A classic dystopian novel that imagines a futuristic world where a totalitarian government controls society through conditioning, drugs, and genetic engineering to maintain stability.

★★★★★

The Boy and the Samurai

A historical fiction novel set in 16th-century Japan, following a young boy who survives a shipwreck and is taken in by a kind-hearted samurai.

The Eleventh Plague

A young adult post-apocalyptic novel that follows a teenage boy navigating a world ravaged by a plague, learning to survive and find hope in a broken society.

Ender's Game

by Orson Scott Card

A science fiction military novel where a young genius is recruited to a space academy to be trained to lead a future war against an alien race.
